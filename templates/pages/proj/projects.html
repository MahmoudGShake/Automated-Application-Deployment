Previous Projects

Before the inception of DIC, the founding members intensively worked on research, development, and innovation (R+D+I) projects. They had +20 years of academic and industrial experience and led multiple European and International projects, such as:

  • "Intelligent system for detecting defects in textiles (i-Detective)", a project funded by The Technology Agency of the Czech Republic (TAČR).
  • "Development of a smart shape-memory sensor for biological pressure monitoring applications (SmartShape)", a project funded by Horizon Europe.
  • "Smart Textile foR hEAlth Monitoring (STREAM)", a project funded by the European Research Executive Agency (REA) under the Horizon2020 Program of MSCA-IF.
  • "inherently Flexible Aerogels for energy effiCient structurES (i-FACES)", a project funded by the European Economic Area (EEA) and Norway Grants.
  • "Ceramics with sensing capabilities for high temperature applications (CENTAUR)", a project funded by the M-ERA.NET European Program.
  • "micro-struCtural imaging as a Tool for modelinG fibrOUs materiALS (μ-CT GOALS)", a project funded by the Czech Ministry of Education, Youth and Sports (MŠMT).
  • "Alliance for Advanced Manufacturing in Central Europe (AMiCE)", a project funded by the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), Interreg Central Europe.
  • "An Automated System for Fabric Faults Inspection to Enhance Textile Handicrafts", a project funded by the Egyptian Academy of Scientific Research.

Besides the above projects coordinated by DigInnoCent members (before the company's inception), we also participated in many R+D+I projects such as:

  • "Stress mapping around SWNT and MWNT in polymer-matrix nano-composites", a project funded by U.S. Department of Commerce.
  • "Efficient biological-chemical protective materials", a project funded by U.S. Department of Homeland Security and the U.S. National Textile Center (NTC).
  • "Studying the interaction relations between fibers and yarns", a project funded by the Egyptian Scientific Research Academy.
  • "Evaluation of Structures Movements using Structural Health Monitoring and AI-based Machine Learning", Ministry of Education, Saudi Arabia.

Consultancy Services for Researchers

At DIC, we have recently developed an innovative digital system called PulseHub. This platform streamlines the project preparation process, making it more efficient, collaborative, and transparent. PulseHub offers a range of features, including task management, document sharing, version control, and real-time collaboration tools. By leveraging this cutting-edge system, we ensure an organised and seamless workflow throughout the entire proposal development process.
